PRR forest plot
PRR Signal Confirmed: Semaglutide PRR 3.20 (CI 2.91–3.52), Tirzepatide 2.80 (CI 2.41–3.25), Liraglutide 2.10 (CI 1.78–2.48) all exceed WHO-UMC threshold. χ² all > 10, p < 0.001.
Weight Finding: Patients with severe GI events weighed significantly more (median 98 kg vs 87 kg, p < 0.001). Each 10 kg increase associated with ~8% higher odds of severe GI event.
Polypharmacy: ≥ 8 concurrent medications is the strongest modifiable risk factor (OR 2.4, 95% CI 2.1–2.8). Represents ~12% of GLP-1 cohort but 27% of severe outcomes.

Model Metrics Comparison

Key classification metrics at recall-optimized threshold (0.35)
Model metrics comparison
Random Forest (AUC 0.843): Superior recall (0.79 vs 0.61) — critical for pharmacovigilance. Missing a high-risk patient is costlier than a false alert. 100 trees, max depth 12, SMOTE oversampling.
Logistic Regression (AUC 0.720): Interpretable baseline. Key: concurrent opioid OR ≈ 3.2, polypharmacy OR ≈ 1.18/drug, age 65–74 OR ≈ 2.1. All significant at p < 0.001.
Threshold: Set at 0.35 (vs default 0.50) to maximize recall. In medical surveillance, false negatives carry disproportionate cost.
